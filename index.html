Introduction

Studies from World Health Organization (WHO) points that “one in four people will be affected by mental or neurological disorders at some point in their lives” (World Health Organization, Media Centre. 2001). Years after this conclusion, other studies also carried out by WHO, alerts that "depression is the leading cause of ill health and disability worldwide” (World Health Organization, “Depression: Let’s Talk”. 03 March. 2017). These facts are again highlighted in recent statistics that show that “in every 40 seconds, someone loses their life to suicide” (World Health Organization, Mental Health Day. 2019), what means that the subject has been increased by the time, and bringing other consequences to the scenario.

Another study pointed by the WHO explains that the main symptoms of someone dealing with mental issues are withdrawal from friends, activities and social life in general. The reasons for that are vary, but the main one is the fear of rejection or judgment of close people.

Application

Given this sad reality, the OpenApp, a mobile application, was developeded to help in some way to minimize this consequent social isolation. Designed for people, of any gender or age, suffering from mental illness issues, such as depression, anxiety disorders and so on that, in somehow, does not feel comfortable to talk in person to a close friend or family member. Talking about their condition, opening up to someone would be way to avoid any worst behavior from them and a good start to seek and get help.

Function

The main function of OpenApp is provide the connection between the profiles registered in the app. Profiles are classified as Users, designed for people that is seeking for support, and Helpers, designed for people wants to help. These options are available during the Sign In. Finally, the connections between Users and Helpers are made by matching their preferences. Once the match between profiles occurs, then they are ready to interact to each other.

Preferences

During the Sign In, basic information is request in order to register the profile and create an account in the app. One of the information requested is about the Preferences, that straight foward refers to the User’s preferences to connect with another person. These information are: Age - picking up a range of age or none, Gender - choosing between Female, Male, Other or none, and Location - specifying a point of distance from the User or none. of a person with whom the User prefers to connect. These information will be automatically compared with other registered profiles, and once these preferences match the profiles, then both profiles will be available and visible between them.

Match

After create the profile and login to the account, the User is redirected to the Feed, where other profiles that match the User's preferences are displayed. Each profile is shown, one at time, in cards. To choose a profile, the User has to swipe right the card. To check the next profile, then the User has to swipe left the card, and a new profile will be displayed on the Feed screen.

Features

OpenApp presents users a mobile application with a series of resources in order to create a good experience for end-users. Among these features are an intuitive and easy navigation, responsive design, search options, user feedback collection, social integration to allow users to sign up, like, comment or share their content and private messages.

In addition to the features, a list of Daily Tasks were developed in regards to improve the user engagement. A user can write on virtual daily journal called Day Book, or even record a short video or audio message about their feelings or thoughts, by using the Log CAM or Voice LOG. Those features would be used to track their emotional state by the time.

Another way to promote the interaction between the profiles is made by Forum, where Users and Helpes could create their own Forum, specifying the subject, rules and control who could join in it.
